The Vampire Woman

This is the article about Maria The Vampire Woman of Tony Permadi's website: Tony.Permadi.id. It contains information of a Mexican woman who likes to modify her body parts to look like a vampire: María José Cristerna Méndez.

Well known as "La Mujer Vampiro" or The Vampire Woman, Maria Jose Cristerna Mendez prefers to be called The Jaguar Woman. She has been listed in Guinness World Records book since 2012 as "the most modified female" in the world, with 96% of her skin is tattooed and 49 body implants, including a range of transdermal implants on her forehead, chest, and arms, as well as multiple piercings all over her body.

María José Cristerna Méndez, "The Vampire Woman."

"The Vampire Woman" owes her nickname to a Mexican TV program that found a similarity between her looks and a vampire because of her implanted sharp, vampiric fangs. She also altered the black pigmentation of her eyes and implanted multiple titanium horns in her head.

Her looks that is similar to that of a vampire.

María José (born in 1976) is a mother of four currently residing in Zapopan, Mexico. She grew up in a religious Catholic family and had her first tattoo of the logo of a Swedish metal band called Bathory when she was 14. It is like the Sabbatic Goat known as Baphomet, a good and evil mystical stuff. The word Bathory is related to Transylvania's "Blood Countess" Elizabeth Báthory, purported to be a witch who liked to bathe in blood to maintain her youth.

María José in her teenage years.

She got married when she was 17. Since then, her husband forbade her from tattooing. Maria began modifying her body parts with implants after she ended her 10-year marriage. She admitted that her marriage was an abusive relationship and that "tattoos were a form of liberation from me." She freed herself and gave free rein to her creativity as a tattoo artist.

Her appearance when got married and just after divorced.

María, who studied criminal law at Universidad del Valle de Atemajac and worked as a lawyer for a while, said that the titanium horns she has are a symbol of strength. She had them implanted without anesthesia. She changed the color of her eyes because it was how she wanted to be. Her fangs exist because "I have loved vampires since I was a kid," she said.

Titanium horn implants in her forehead.

She has a tattoo on her jaw that the women of the Māori tribe did that also symbolizes strength. She tattooed stars all over her face when her mother died. "I tattooed stars because she is in the sky in one of them," she said. She plans to have two more pieces of titanium horns implanted in the back of her head and described her tattooing as a "form of liberation" after suffering domestic violence.

Her looks with a little change in her long, red hair.

She has her favorites among those tattoos on her body. Some dolls that symbolize her happy childhood, a broken heart that reminds her of the death of her parents, and the eyes of her four children that she has tattooed on her right leg. The stars on her face are also her favorite.

Various kinds of tattoos on both of María José's legs.

Maria Jose has been a special guest in many national and international TV programs and events like the International Convention of Tattoo Artists in Bogota, Colombia, Venezuela Expo Tattoo in Caracas, Venezuela, and the National Geographic Channel's "Taboo" TV proram. Her stunning looks made Ripley's Believe It or Not! decide to have a wax statue of her in all of its museums around the world.

A wax statue of her at Ripley's museum in Blackpool, England.

With her looks, Maria cannot disguise herself. She always attracts people's attention in the streets. The simplest thing that might involve going out, like taking out the garbage or going to the farmer's market, can become quite an ordeal. She never goes anywhere unnoticed.

She always makes people nervous around her.

She is running her business of tattoo parlors: Adicción Corporal 1 and 2. She has a boutique where she sells her clothing line of "Mujeres Vampiro" (Vampire Women) and other Gothic fashion clothes and accessories. "Tattooing is my way of being immortal, of really being a vampire and not dying - leaving my work on other people's skin," she said.

María José in her tattoo parlor serving the customers.
